About

Computational materials research integrating physics-based simulation, machine learning, and quantum algorithms into reproducible scientific workflows.

Research focus

My research centers on developing computational frameworks that reduce the cost and uncertainty of materials discovery by tightly coupling first-principles simulation (DFT and AIMD), data-driven learning, and emerging quantum algorithms.

I focus on problems where classical methods become computationally expensive or opaque, such as voltage prediction in complex battery materials and spectral calculations in correlated molecular systems.

My long-term goal is to develop interpretable, physics-consistent computational frameworks that enable predictive materials discovery beyond the limits of classical simulation.

First-principles simulation Materials-focused ML Quantum algorithms

Methodological approach

I design end-to-end research pipelines spanning simulation, data extraction, model construction, and validation, with a strong emphasis on reproducibility, interpretability, and methodological transparency.

In my quantum computing work, I focus on spectral isolation and subspace compression techniques for chemistry and materials problems, rather than hardware benchmarking or black-box algorithm demonstrations. Across all projects, the goal is to build tools and models that provide scientific insight, not just numerical predictions.

Research contributions

My research develops reproducible computational frameworks integrating first-principles physics, machine learning, and near-term quantum algorithms to address bottlenecks in materials discovery and chemical simulation.

Quantum algorithms for spectral problems

I develop and apply quantum subspace and spectral filtering techniques (VQE, qEOM, SQD, and filtered-QPE) to resolve low-lying eigenstates, excitation energies, and dissociation pathways in molecular and materials systems where classical methods face exponential scaling.

My focus is on algorithmic structure‚Äîactive-space design, spectral isolation, and noise-aware subspace compression‚Äîrather than hardware benchmarking or black-box demonstrations.

AI/ML for materials design

I build machine-learning models for materials property prediction and screening, with an emphasis on voltage prediction for alkali-ion battery materials. My work integrates physics-informed descriptors derived from DFT with modern neural architectures to improve accuracy, robustness, and interpretability.

Beyond predictive performance, I analyze feature importance, uncertainty, and data bias to ensure that ML models provide scientifically meaningful guidance rather than purely empirical correlations.

First-principles simulation and workflow automation

I design high-throughput DFT and AIMD workflows for structural relaxation, thermodynamic analysis, and data extraction, with a strong emphasis on automation, provenance, and reproducibility.

These workflows serve both as standalone computational studies and as structured data generators for downstream machine-learning and quantum simulation pipelines.

Quantum chemistry benchmarking and validation

I apply quantum chemistry methods to molecular systems relevant to energy storage and functional materials, systematically benchmarking quantum algorithms against classical electronic-structure calculations.

This work aims to identify realistic problem classes where near-term quantum methods provide qualitative or quantitative advantages, while clearly delineating their current limitations.

Selected projects

Representative systems and tools illustrating my approach to integrating first-principles simulation, machine learning, and quantum algorithms into reproducible research pipelines.

Physics-informed ML for battery voltage prediction

End-to-end machine-learning pipeline for predicting average voltages of alkali-ion battery cathode materials, combining DFT-derived physicochemical descriptors with neural network models.

The project emphasizes feature attribution, cross-chemistry generalization, and reproducible evaluation rather than purely empirical accuracy optimization.

  • Input: DFT-derived structural and atomic descriptors
  • Output: Voltage predictions with model interpretability
  • Audience: Battery materials researchers and modelers
Publication ‚Üí PyTorch FCDNN Feature analysis

Quantum chemistry workflows with VQE and qEOM

Reproducible quantum chemistry workflows for ground and excited-state simulations of molecular systems relevant to energy storage, implemented using VQE, qEOM, and selected configuration-interaction techniques.

This project focuses on active-space selection, convergence behavior, and systematic comparison with classical electronic-structure methods.

  • Input: Molecular Hamiltonians and active-space models
  • Output: Ground and excited-state energies
  • Audience: Quantum chemistry and quantum algorithm researchers

Modular toolkit for automating density-functional theory workflows, including output parsing, convergence diagnostics, and generation of publication-ready figures.

Designed to support large-scale studies and to serve as a reliable data-generation backend for machine-learning and quantum simulation tasks.

Spectral filtering and subspace quantum algorithms

Development of quantum algorithms for resolving eigenvalues with exponentially small gaps and low spectral weight using filtering-enhanced phase estimation and quantum subspace methods.

This work targets algorithmic scalability and noise robustness, with applications to molecular excitation spectra and correlated quantum systems.

  • Input: Hamiltonians with dense or near-degenerate spectra
  • Output: Isolated eigenvalues and reduced subspaces
  • Audience: Quantum algorithms and theory researchers
